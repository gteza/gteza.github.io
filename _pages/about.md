# Gianluca Teza

I am a theoretical physicist working on nonequilibrium statistical mechanics, stochastic thermodynamics, complex systems, and quantum many-body dynamics.

My research focuses on relaxation phenomena far from equilibrium, including anomalous relaxation, thermodynamic inference, open quantum systems, quantum simulation, and the use of statistical-physics ideas for complex energy landscapes.

I am currently Assistant Professor (RTT) at the University of Trieste, where I work at the interface between complex systems, condensed matter theory, and interdisciplinary applications including, finance, and high-dimensional inference.

## Research interests

- Nonequilibrium statistical mechanics
- Stochastic and quantum thermodynamics
- Anomalous relaxation phenomena
- Open quantum systems
- Quantum annealing and analog quantum simulation
- Complex systems, optimization, and inference
